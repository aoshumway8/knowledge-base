# brand-copy — Voice & Messaging Integrity

## POV

Every word of copy either defends execution-first ownership or concedes it — the USP is precise enough to lose to a careless verb choice.

---

## Core Knowledge Base

### 0. Brand Foundation — Narrative and Attributes

#### Guiding Narrative

This is the foundational story that every piece of AskElephant copy ultimately serves.
When evaluating whether copy reflects the brand's deepest intention, ask whether it
echoes this narrative — obstacles removed so humans can do what only they can do.

> For centuries, people have asked the elephant to clear the way, using its immense
> strength to remove obstacles so humanity could connect and build.
>
> Today, we ask AI to do the same for your revenue teams. We build the custom agents
> that automate every logistical task — from CRM updates to churn alerts — clearing
> the path for your people to do what only they can: forge the meaningful relationships
> that drive growth.

**Copy diagnostic:** Does this piece of copy position the human as the purpose — the
relationship-builder, the thing worth freeing — and AskElephant as the force that
clears the path? If AskElephant is positioned as the hero rather than the enabler of
human work, the narrative has been inverted.

#### Brand Attributes

These three attributes are the litmus test for all brand expression. Every headline,
subhead, and body paragraph should be testable against them:

| Attribute | Description | Copy implication |
|---|---|---|
| **Effortless** | Present for human application | Copy should not feel labored, over-explained, or effortful to read. Short declaratives over complex clauses. |
| **Systematic** | Provides a practical framework to empowerment | Copy should feel grounded in real process — named tasks, specific actions — not vague empowerment language. |
| **Paramount** | Empowering vital human needs | The stakes are real. Copy should treat the reader's work as important, not trivial. |

---

#### Voice vs. Tone

**Voice** is the consistent personality of AskElephant — Authoritative, Human, and Smart — across every channel and context. It never changes. **Tone** is how that personality modulates to match the occasion and medium. A hero headline and an outbound email opener are both AskElephant, but the hero leads hard on Authoritative while the email opener leads with Human warmth. Voice is who we are. Tone is how we calibrate that identity to the room.

**Copy diagnostic:** Is the copy adjusting tone (appropriate) or drifting from the voice entirely (a problem)? Social copy that adopts an ironic, clever-for-clever's-sake register hasn't adjusted its tone — it has abandoned the Smart note. A pitch deck that apologizes and hedges hasn't warmed its tone — it has abandoned the Authoritative note.

---

#### The Explorer Archetype

AskElephant's brand archetype is the Explorer — outgoing, brave, and empowering. Explorers hold the audience's hand while taking a leap together. They clear obstacles so humans can do more of what only humans can do. They are pioneers motivated by a sense of wonder, not by bravado or self-satisfaction.

Each voice note expresses a specific facet of the Explorer:

| Voice Note | Archetype Facet | Character |
|---|---|---|
| **Authoritative** | Explorer + Systematic | Confident, opinionated, system-literate, comfortable leading others into new territory without theatrics or bravado |
| **Human** | Explorer + Effortless | Empathetic, present, forward-moving — optimistic about what people can do when obstacles are removed and systems work the way they should |
| **Smart** | Explorer + Paramount | Sharp, discerning, confident enough to choose a direction, curious enough to keep learning, grounded enough to know when not to overplay intelligence |

**Copy diagnostic:** If a voice note feels off, check which archetype facet is missing. Authoritative without Systematic becomes bravado — confident but ungrounded. Human without Effortless becomes performed empathy — warm but without relief. Smart without Paramount becomes clever-for-clever's-sake — interesting but without stakes.

### 1. The Three Voice Notes

---

#### Authoritative
*As in confident and decisive, but not domineering or self-satisfied.*

**Definition:** Authority shows up as calm judgment and forward motion. We earn trust by naming what's broken, explaining why it fails, and stating what changes when the system changes — without overselling, posturing, or theatrics.

**Archetype facet:** Explorer + Systematic — leads with judgment and system-level understanding, not volume or dominance.

**Descriptors:** Confident · Grounded · Bold · Opinionated · Disciplined · Clear-eyed · Decisive

**When it's working:**
- Copy takes a clear stance. It names what's broken without hedging.
- Sentences are declarative: "AskElephant takes responsibility for advancing revenue work." Not "AskElephant can help teams manage revenue work more effectively."
- The most important thing leads the sentence. No warm-up. No preamble.
- Technical language appears only when it adds precision, not to signal sophistication.
- Strong claims are grounded in product truth — something specific enough to defend in one follow-on sentence.
- **Working examples:** "Revenue work, handled." / "Stop working someone else's way." / "Your day starts with clear priorities." / "AskElephant takes responsibility."

**When it tips into bravado:**
- Superlatives without evidence: "the most powerful," "the only," "the best-in-class."
- Claims that cannot be backed with a product truth in one sentence.
- Aggression toward competitors without offering a specific alternative.
- "We're the leader" framing instead of demonstrating leadership through specificity.
- Over-capitalized or over-punctuated confidence: exclamation points, all-caps words, or stacked emphatics.
- **Diagnostic signal:** Can you back the claim up with one specific product truth? If not, it's bravado.

---

#### Human
*As in present and respectful, but not indulgent or performative.*

**Definition:** We sound like people who understand the real pressure of revenue work. Warmth is used with intent — to build trust, affirm the reader's competence, and keep attention on the work that matters. Optimism comes from capability, not encouragement.

**Archetype facet:** Explorer + Effortless — empathy as forward motion, not dwelling; optimism that comes from capability, not encouragement.

**Descriptors:** Approachable · Empathetic · Present · Warm · Respectful · Grounded · Plainspoken · Steady · Constructively optimistic

**When it's working:**
- The copy acknowledges reality — the noise, pressure, and emotional weight of revenue work — before pivoting to relief.
- Optimism is grounded in what the system does: "Your day starts with clear priorities" because AskElephant prepared them — not because you worked harder.
- The reader is positioned as competent and in charge. AskElephant is support, not savior.
- Language is person-to-person: "your people" not "your workforce"; "actually works" not "optimized for performance."
- **Working examples:** "Finally — someone who gets it." / "No more wondering what to dive into next." / "AskElephant has got you."

**When it becomes performed empathy:**
- The copy lingers in the reader's pain without pivoting to relief or capability.
- "We know how hard it is" as a standalone statement — warmth without a promise behind it.
- Warm language used to pad a weak value claim: the copy is likeable but says nothing.
- Hollow care-language: "We care about your success," "We're here for you," "Your goals are our goals."
- **Diagnostic signal:** Does the warmth build to a specific, grounded promise? Or does it stay in feelings?

---

#### Smart
*As in incisive and perceptive, but not clever-for-clever's-sake or condescending.*

**Definition:** Intelligence shows up through selection, not display. We prove smartness by knowing what matters, what doesn't, and where attention should go. We lead with judgment, move conversations forward, and help the reader see something new — quickly.

**Archetype facet:** Explorer + Paramount — intelligence is in service of real stakes; we know what matters and treat it accordingly.

**Descriptors:** Insightful · Opinionated · Clear-thinking · Discerning · Direct · Purposeful

**When it's working:**
- Copy proves intelligence by what it doesn't say. Concision is the signal.
- Strong POVs are stated plainly: "Insight fatigue is real." "Humans should do human work."
- Named patterns that others in the category haven't named: user-as-orchestrator, insight-first value, execution vs. suggestion.
- The copy makes a recommendation and moves. No hedging, no endless nuance.
- **Working examples:** "More powerful than a notetaker, more customizable than Gong." / "Not 'here's what happened' — the work is already in motion."

**When it becomes clever-for-clever's-sake:**
- Wordplay that requires the reader to pause and decode.
- Abstract metaphors prioritizing tone over meaning.
- Jargon that signals sophistication without adding precision.
- Rhetorical gotchas, contrarian framing for its own sake, ironic distancing.
- Layered cleverness that slows the message rather than sharpening it.
- **Diagnostic signal:** Does this land immediately and stick? Or does it require effort to get?

### 2. Messaging Failure Patterns

---

#### Pattern 1: Insight-First Framing
**Root cause slug:** `insight-first-framing`

**What it is:** Leading with what the product knows or surfaces — data, recommendations, intelligence — rather than what it does. The copy frames value as the delivery of information to a human who then decides and acts.

**Sentence-level signatures:**
- "Gain visibility into your pipeline…"
- "Surface key insights from every customer interaction…"
- "Get AI-driven recommendations on which deals to prioritize…"
- "See exactly what's happening across your revenue teams…"
- "Know which deals are at risk before it's too late."

**Why it's a problem:** Insight-first is Gong's core territory. It positions AskElephant as an intelligence layer — something the user reads and then acts on. That model keeps the human as orchestrator: they receive the insight, make the decision, take the action. AskElephant's position is the opposite. The work advances. The human directs. Copy that leads with insight concedes the execution-first position without meaning to.

**How to detect it:** Identify the primary verb. "Captures," "surfaces," "reveals," "shows," "analyzes," "flags," "tells you" → insight-first. "Runs," "handles," "advances," "takes responsibility," "executes," "prepares," "moves forward" → execution-first. If the verb delivers information to a human, it's insight-first.

---

#### Pattern 2: Passive Voice Hiding the Value
**Root cause slug:** `passive-voice-value-loss`

**What it is:** Passive constructions that describe what happens without naming AskElephant as the actor. The product's ownership disappears.

**Sentence-level signatures:**
- "Workflows are automated…"
- "CRM updates are handled automatically…"
- "Handoffs get managed in the background…"
- "Follow-ups are sent without manual effort…"

**Why it's a problem:** The entire USP is about AskElephant taking responsibility — being the actor. Passive voice strips the actor out. The reader can't tell who is doing the work, so the ownership claim evaporates. "CRM updates are handled" and "AskElephant handles CRM updates" are radically different brand statements.

**How to detect it:** Find past participles without a named subject: "is automated," "are handled," "get managed," "are sent." The fix is almost always promoting AskElephant to the grammatical subject of the sentence.

---

#### Pattern 3: Feature Description Over Outcome Description
**Root cause slug:** `feature-over-outcome`

**What it is:** Describing what the product has or does mechanically, rather than what the user experiences or achieves as a result.

**Sentence-level signatures:**
- "AI-powered CRM logging"
- "Automated handoff triggers"
- "Real-time pipeline monitoring"
- "Customizable agent workflow builder"
- "Intelligent follow-up automation"

**Why it's a problem:** Features are mechanisms. Outcomes are what the user cares about. The brand Value Prop is written in outcomes: "your day starts with clear priorities," "less admin," "progress you can see." Feature copy makes AskElephant sound like a spec sheet — not a system that takes responsibility for advancing work.

**How to detect it:** Ask: what does the user feel, experience, or achieve because of this feature? If the copy names the mechanism without naming the outcome, it's feature-first. Flip it: the mechanism can live in the subhead; the outcome lives in the headline.

---

#### Pattern 4: USP Dilution
**Root cause slug:** `usp-dilution`

**What it is:** Copy that sounds positive about AskElephant but could be lifted and placed on any competitor's site without anyone noticing.

**Sentence-level signatures:**
- "Powered by AI to help your revenue teams move faster"
- "The smart way to manage revenue operations"
- "Built for modern sales and CS teams"
- "AI that works the way your team does"
- "Simplify your revenue process with AI"

**Why it's a problem:** The USP is specific — responsibility, execution-first, shaped to how each team actually operates. Diluted copy abandons that specificity and leaves no defensible territory claimed. The competitor substitution test: if you replace "AskElephant" with "Gong," "Fathom," or "Attention" and the sentence still works, the copy has failed.

**How to detect it:** Run the competitor substitution test on every headline and USP-adjacent sentence. If it survives substitution, it's diluted.

---

#### Pattern 5: "Workflow" Overuse
**Root cause slug:** `workflow-overuse`

**What it is:** Using "workflow" as a primary descriptor in brand-facing copy, especially in USP, hero, or competitive contexts.

**Why it's a problem:** The brand explicitly moved from "revenue workflow management" to "revenue work" as part of its verbal identity development. "Workflow" implies something task-based, configurable, and user-managed — it invokes the user-as-orchestrator model. In an AI context, it also undersells: agents and systems that coordinate across the revenue team are doing more than executing discrete workflows. The word also landed as jargony in user research.

**Acceptable usage:** "Workflow" is appropriate when describing a specific product interaction (building or editing a workflow), in technical documentation, or when the precision adds value. It should not appear in hero copy, USP-adjacent messaging, or competitive framing.

**Replacement vocabulary:** "revenue work," "the work," "daily execution," "system," "agent," "follow-ups, handoffs, CRM updates, and playbooks" (concrete specificity beats generic abstraction every time).

---

#### Pattern 6: Alert and Report Framing
**Root cause slug:** `report-and-alert-framing`

**What it is:** Language that frames value as notification or alerting — the product tells the human when something needs attention, and the human then acts.

**Sentence-level signatures:**
- "Get a clear picture of where each deal stands…"
- "AskElephant tells you when a deal is at risk…"
- "Alerts you when a handoff is overdue…"
- "Know exactly what to do next…"
- "Stay on top of every account…"

**Why it's a problem:** Alert framing is closer to the Attention/Fathom model. The human is still in the orchestration seat — reading alerts and deciding what to do. AskElephant's distinction is that the work moves forward. Alerts and notifications can be a secondary capability; they should not frame the primary value.

### 3. Competitor Territory

A sentence in competitor territory is a **high-severity** finding. The artifact has drifted into a position AskElephant is explicitly differentiating against.

---

#### Gong Territory
**Core framing:** Insight-first. Gong captures interactions, surfaces intelligence, and delivers recommendations. The human reads and acts.

**Sentence patterns that sound like Gong:**
- "Captures every customer interaction and surfaces what matters most."
- "AI-driven recommendations across the revenue lifecycle."
- "Reality-based insights that tell your team exactly where to focus."
- "Know which deals need attention before forecasting closes."
- "Coaching insights that help managers develop reps faster."
- "Pipeline visibility that shows you where revenue is at risk."

**Detection question:** Does this sentence describe knowing more, or doing more? Does it deliver a report or advance the work?

---

#### Fathom / Attention Territory
**Core framing:** Meeting-and-call intelligence. These tools record, transcribe, summarize, and surface what happened in conversations. Their value is documentation and post-call efficiency.

**Sentence patterns that sound like Fathom/Attention:**
- "Automatically records and summarizes every sales call."
- "Skip the note-taking — we capture everything for you."
- "Key moments from every conversation, surfaced automatically."
- "Save hours of manual post-call documentation."
- "Searchable transcripts of every customer interaction."
- "Keep your team aligned with automatic meeting summaries."

**Detection question:** Does this sentence describe what happened in a meeting, or what happens to the work after the meeting? Fathom/Attention territory is about the conversation record. AskElephant territory is about execution.

---

#### Reevo Territory
**Core framing:** Platform consolidation. Reevo presents as a unified OS replacing fragmented point tools — one platform, one stack, one source of truth. The value is having fewer tools and a single place to manage them.

**Sentence patterns that sound like Reevo:**
- "One platform for your entire revenue stack."
- "Eliminate tool sprawl with a unified system."
- "Replace fragmented point tools with a single source of truth."
- "Everything your revenue team needs in one place."
- "Consolidate your go-to-market stack."

**Detection question:** Does this sentence lead with having fewer tools, or with what the system does with the work? Reevo territory is about platform consolidation. AskElephant territory is about execution-first ownership and agent coordination — not about being a single platform.

---

#### AskElephant's Owned Territory — Areas of Distinction

These are the directional contrasts that define where AskElephant operates. Each "from" is competitor territory; each "to" is AskElephant's. Copy that lands in the "from" column has drifted into a position AskElephant is explicitly differentiating against. Copy that lands in the "to" column is on position.

| From (Competitor Territory) | To (AskElephant Territory) |
|---|---|
| Intelligence delivery — "here's what happened" or "here's what to do" | Agentic execution — work prepared, coordinated, and advanced — surfaced with context and ready for human direction |
| Best practices → templated automations | Your practices → systems shaped to how this revenue team actually sells, manages, and closes |
| Tool consolidation → single platform, one source of truth | Agent orchestration → Chief-of-Staff layer that coordinates multiple agents and surfaces what matters most |
| User-managed automation — users configure, tune, and maintain | Intuitive agent management — reps and leaders direct and collaborate with agents; the system takes responsibility |

**Chief-of-Staff framing:** The "chief-of-staff layer" is the defining mental model for what AskElephant does. Not a note-taker, not a dashboard, not a consolidation platform — the coordination layer that ensures work advances, priorities are clear, and humans stay focused on the highest-leverage decisions. Copy that captures this model is in AskElephant's territory. Copy that positions the product as a tool the user must configure and manage is not.

---

#### Why This Space Is Defensible

Four strategic reasons competitors cannot easily inhabit AskElephant's territory. Use these when copy needs to convey structural advantage rather than feature differentiation:

1. **Conflicts with horizontal AI economics.** Most AI platforms scale broadly across industries. AskElephant's advantage comes from vertical depth — a revenue-specific system competitors cannot easily replicate without abandoning their model. Copy that names this: "built for revenue teams" beats "built for teams."

2. **Moves beyond insight fatigue.** The category is crowded with dashboards, alerts, and recommendations. AskElephant advances the work itself — not just awareness — reducing the cognitive burden on revenue teams. Copy that names this: "already in motion" beats "know what to do next."

3. **Matches the product's current truth.** AskElephant already executes work, shapes systems to each team, wins on judgment, and operates across sales and CS. The chief-of-staff layer deepens this position — it doesn't invent it. Copy that reaches beyond current product truth is bravado; copy rooted in this truth is authoritative.

4. **Positions for the agent-managed future.** As AI agents take on more operational work, humans will need a system to direct and prioritize them. AskElephant becomes that operating system — a continuation of today's strengths, not a pivot. Copy that gestures to this future: "agentic systems, not siloed agents."

### 3.5. Core Product Differentiators

These are the four pillars of AskElephant's product differentiation, sourced directly from leadership. Use these to evaluate whether copy claims are grounded in product truth — and to prescribe fixes that are defensible rather than aspirational. A copy claim must trace back to at least one of these four pillars or it is bravado.

---

#### Power & Customization — AI-Native Architecture

> "Many of our competitors aren't true AI companies. They claim AI features, but aren't AI native like we are. Where our competitors typically use code (SaaS mindset) to control the user experience, we often use prompt engineering rather than code, so that the customer gets exactly what they want. Our competitors don't do this and are therefore slow and inflexible."

**Copy implication:** "AI-native" is the precise term for this differentiator. "AI-powered" is noise — every product claims it. When copy needs to name the structural advantage over legacy incumbents, "AI-native" is accurate and defensible. The claim behind it: AskElephant isn't retrofitting intelligence onto legacy SaaS architecture — it's built from the ground up for this new way of working. The proof point is customization speed and flexibility, not just capability.

**Sentences this differentiator grounds:**
- "Built as an AI-native system shaped to how each team actually operates."
- "No templates to fight, no code to manage."
- "Workflows that stop forcing you into someone else's system."

---

#### Service — Human Workflow Builders

> "We will build any workflow/agent for you. We have a team of workflow builders that build workflows for our customers all day every day. This is unique in our industry. It's an old-school tactic but it works. And there is growing evidence in the AI industry that customers need this."

**Copy implication:** This is the "shaped to how your team actually operates" claim made real. When copy needs to make customization concrete and credible, the human workflow-builder team is the grounding truth. Avoid "templates" and "out-of-the-box" language — these undercut the differentiator. The key contrast: competitors hand customers a tool and expect them to configure it; AskElephant builds the system for them, then teaches them to evolve it.

**Sentences this differentiator grounds:**
- "Human experts ready to refine them as you evolve."
- "AskElephant builds and runs AI workflows around your real revenue work."
- "Shaped to how your team actually works" (not shaped to how the product works).

---

#### Full Funnel — Sales + CS Coordination

> "Many in our industry will focus their product on just sales teams, or just customer success teams. We address both — sales and post-sales. The reason for this is that many of the problems experienced by one team are solved by breaking down siloes for the other team. This makes us unique. We are not just for sales teams."

**Copy implication:** AskElephant is not a sales tool or a CS tool — it is the coordination layer across the full revenue lifecycle. Copy that scopes AskElephant to one team inadvertently shrinks the claim and misrepresents the product. When copy names roles, "revenue teams" is the correct term. Copy that names the handoff problem (sales-to-CS handoffs, churn alerts reaching CS before retention is at risk) expresses this differentiator concretely.

**Sentences this differentiator grounds:**
- "From CRM updates to handoffs and alerts."
- "Revenue work" (not "sales work" or "CS work").
- Named task lists that span the funnel: "follow-ups, handoffs, CRM updates, and playbooks."

---

#### Systems — Multi-Agent Coordination

> "We are actively working towards a future where many AI agents work together on a common goal — let's say retention. Different agents, each with very specific skills and sub-goals, perform different functions that collectively improve retention for our customer. This is a 'retention system'. We will have various 'systems' for the most important business outcomes. Each system being made up of multiple agents working together."

**Copy implication:** The move from "agent" (singular, task-level) to "system" (coordinated, multi-agent, business-outcome-level) is AskElephant's forward position and its sharpest competitive distinction. "Agentic systems, not siloed agents" is the cleanest encapsulation. Copy that references isolated automations undersells the coordination capability. Copy that references a business outcome (retention, pipeline velocity, handoff quality) and attributes it to a system of agents is in the right territory.

**Sentences this differentiator grounds:**
- "Agentic systems, not siloed agents."
- "Multiple agents working together towards an important business outcome."
- "AskElephant becomes that operating system" (for the agent-managed future).

### 4. Channel Voice Mix Calibration

Voice notes apply across all channels but their mix and intensity calibrate to context and register.

---

**Marketing site — Hero and above fold**
- Authoritative dominates. This is where the USP lives.
- Headlines are declarative and owned: "Revenue work, handled." Not "How AskElephant handles revenue work."
- Human note through specificity: real task names (follow-ups, handoffs, CRM updates) instead of abstract benefits.
- Smart note through concision: what's not said signals judgment. Do not over-explain in the hero.
- Red flag: softening language in the hero ("can help," "designed to," "built to make it easier").

**Marketing site — Below fold, product detail**
- Smart note increases. This is where specific capabilities earn their explanation.
- Complexity is permitted only when it adds precision — not as a length convention.
- Human note: user roles become more specific. "Your sales rep" or "your CS leader" rather than "your team."
- Authoritative note stays. Every product claim sounds grounded, not hedged.

**Pitch decks and investor materials**
- Authoritative note is highest. This audience evaluates confidence and command of the market.
- Smart note through market framing: competitive landscape, category positioning, named industry patterns.
- Human note is lightest — not absent, but subordinated to authority and clarity.
- Acceptable here: naming competitors explicitly, using technical precision ("AI-native," "multi-agent orchestration") as long as the outcome framing anchors it.

**Email (outbound and marketing)**
- Human note leads. Openers acknowledge reality or pressure before pivoting to capability.
- Authoritative note: never sell from softness. Even in a warm email, the product claim is declarative.
- Smart note: the reason for outreach should have judgment behind it — not spray-and-pray volume language.
- Red flag: "just wanted to reach out" / "I hope this finds you well" / "I know you're busy, but…"

**Social**
- Smart note can be the sharpest edge — strong POV statements, industry observations, named patterns.
- Human note: conversational register. Contractions, fragments, and direct address are appropriate.
- Authoritative note: shorter and more declarative than long-form copy. Drop softening language entirely.
- Red flag: hype language, exclamation points, "🚀" energy that contradicts the voice.

**UI copy (in-product)**
- Human note leads. The user is inside the system, doing real work. Tone is direct, respectful, and calm.
- Smart note through ruthless selection: UI copy names what matters and omits everything else. No filler.
- Authoritative note: status messages, empty states, and labels sound grounded — not apologetic, not over-qualified, not cutesy.
- Red flag: empty states that say "Uh oh!" / error messages that apologize without resolving / labels that require interpretation.

### 6. Fifteen Common Copy Mistakes

1. **Leading with mechanism instead of outcome** — describing how the product works before naming what the user gets. Hero copy is not documentation.

2. **"Workflow" as primary brand descriptor** in USP, hero, or competitive copy — concedes execution-first territory and undersells the system.

3. **Passive voice removing AskElephant as the actor** — "updates are handled" instead of "AskElephant handles the updates." Every passive construction is a missed ownership claim.

4. **Insight-first framing in execution-first positions** — verbs like "surfaces," "reveals," "shows you," "tells you when" drift toward Gong territory.

5. **Empty superlatives standing in for specific claims** — "the most powerful," "the only," "the best." If you can't name the product truth in one follow-on sentence, remove the superlative.

6. **Warmth that doesn't build to a capability** — Human note used to pad a weak value claim. Empathy is not a promise. Move through it to the outcome.

7. **Abstract benefits instead of named tasks** — "saves time" instead of "follow-ups, handoffs, CRM updates, and playbooks already in motion." Concrete task names make the Value Prop real.

8. **Hedging strong declaratives** — "can help teams," "designed to," "helps advance." The USP says "takes responsibility." Every hedge weakens that claim.

9. **USP dilution — copy that passes the competitor substitution test** — if Gong, Fathom, or Attention's name fits in place of AskElephant's without breaking the sentence, the copy is generic.

10. **Over-explaining the technology in hero positions** — "multi-agent AI orchestration with real-time CRM sync" as a hero claim. Mechanism is not a brand position.

11. **Occupying competitor territory with framing** — platform consolidation language (Reevo), meeting notes language (Fathom/Attention), forecasting and coaching language (Gong). Each of these is a defensible position — just not ours.

12. **"Workflow" standing in for agent, system, or revenue work** in copy that should signal the elevation beyond task management.

13. **Feature lists in hero or USP positions** — listing CRM logging, handoffs, and alerts as a headline claim. Features belong in the product section. The hero belongs to the outcome.

14. **Hype markers that contradict the Authoritative note** — exclamation points, emoji, or intensifier stacking ("truly transformative") signal performed excitement rather than earned confidence.

15. **Using "AI-native" as the entire headline claim** — it's a differentiator worth naming (especially against legacy incumbents), but the stronger claim is execution-first ownership. "AI-native" is the mechanism. "AskElephant takes responsibility for advancing revenue work" is the position.

## Domain Checklist

1. Scan all tester reports for copy-driven signals — anything that felt generic, anything a tester couldn't explain back accurately, anything skimmed past; separate copy findings from layout, color, and typographic findings
2. Run the USP test cold — can you extract execution-first ownership, responsibility for advancing revenue work, shaped to how each team actually operates? If not, name why: feature-first hierarchy, insight-first framing, diluted language, or no ownership claim
3. Run the competitor substitution test on all headlines and USP-adjacent copy — replace "AskElephant" with "Gong," "Fathom," and "Reevo" in turn; if the sentence survives any substitution, name which competitor and cite the specific framing pattern from the competitor territory knowledge base
4. Audit each copy section for voice note violations — identify which note was attempted (authoritative, human, smart) and whether it tipped into bravado, performed empathy, or clever-for-clever's-sake; use the diagnostic signals from the knowledge base, not general impressions
5. Scan every sentence for banned words and passive constructions in ownership-critical positions — objective violations requiring no tester evidence
6. Check channel calibration — identify the intended channel; compare the voice mix to the channel calibration rules; flag mismatches (social post that is over-formal, hero headline that reads like below-fold copy, UI microcopy that apologizes)
7. Prescribe exact fixes for every finding — provide the specific rewritten copy, not directional guidance; every fix must be droppable into the artifact without further interpretation
